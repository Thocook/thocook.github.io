# La Cité — Leasing pipeline

## Project Name
**La Cité — Leasing pipeline**  
**Organization:** La Cité Gatineau  
**Role:** Sales Operations Specialist (contract)  
**Period:** Jul 2024 – Feb 2025  
**Location:** Ottawa / Gatineau (remote)  
**Project Type:** CRM automation, lead operations, booking workflows, reporting

## Portfolio Positioning
This project should be presented as a full sales operations system for a major real estate leasing launch, not just a CRM setup. The work connected marketing channels, website forms, inbound emails, booking flows, Pipedrive CRM, Zapier automations, Calendly, and Power BI reporting into one operational pipeline for residential leasing and commercial interest.

Use this core framing:

> Built and operated a sales operations pipeline for La Cité Gatineau, connecting digital marketing, website forms, inbound emails, Pipedrive CRM, Zapier automations, Calendly booking, and Power BI dashboards into a single lead management and reporting system.

## Project / Business Context
La Cité Gatineau is a modern luxury rental condo and mixed-use development in the heart of Gatineau’s emerging downtown core. The project included residential rental units, office space, and ground-floor commercial space near the cultural, sports, and leisure hub of Gatineau.

The public-facing positioning emphasized:

- Modern and luxury rental condos in the heart of Gatineau.
- Living in the new downtown core of Gatineau.
- A community built around conviviality, camaraderie, and quality of life.
- A 19-storey tower under construction.
- A mixed-use project with residential, office, and commercial space.
- Environmental design elements such as green and white roofs, underground parking, EV charging stations, and landscaped outdoor space.
- A 2.5-acre site with a pedestrian promenade, large terrace, and strong public-realm design.

Portfolio framing:

> The business needed a reliable way to capture, qualify, respond to, book, and report on leads generated by a large real estate launch across digital ads, website forms, inbound email, phone calls, referrals, and offline marketing.

## Business Problem
Before the system was implemented, lead handling was too manual and fragmented. Leads could arrive through many different channels — website forms, booking requests, inbound emails, phone calls, Meta Ads, Google Ads, Google Analytics, and traditional real estate marketing — but there was no single reliable operational pipeline to ensure each lead was captured, categorized, followed up, booked, and reported.

Key pain points:

- Leads were arriving from multiple sources without a single source of truth.
- Manual follow-up created risk of unanswered or delayed leads.
- Website, inbox, phone, ads, and CRM data were not fully connected.
- There was limited visibility into the full funnel from marketing source to showing and lease signing.
- Sales activity was difficult to analyze across lead source, inquiry type, booking status, and follow-up stage.
- Marketing performance needed to connect more directly to CRM and leasing outcomes.

## Solution Overview
Implemented a sales operations infrastructure that connected marketing inputs, CRM workflows, automation logic, booking systems, and reporting dashboards.

Core system components:

- **Pipedrive CRM:** Central system of record for leads, stages, contacts, follow-up tasks, and sales pipeline activity.
- **Zapier:** Automation layer connecting inbound email, website forms, CRM records, booking flows, and follow-up actions.
- **Calendly:** Booking layer for showings and appointments.
- **Website / WordPress:** Lead capture layer through forms, apartment selection, booking requests, and property pages.
- **Outlook / inbound email:** Intake layer for residential and commercial inquiries.
- **Meta Ads / Google Ads / Google Analytics:** Digital marketing and funnel data sources.
- **Power BI:** Reporting layer for marketing, lead, funnel, and sales operations metrics.
- **Database / spreadsheet layer:** Data structure used for storing, cleaning, and combining lead and funnel information.

## System Architecture / Workflow
Simple version:

> Ads / Website / Email / Phone / Referrals → Zapier → Pipedrive CRM → Calendly / Follow-Up Cadences → Power BI Dashboards → Sales and Marketing Decisions

Detailed workflow:

1. Potential renters entered the funnel through digital ads, traditional ads, real estate listings, street walk-ins, website forms, booking requests, inbound emails, or phone calls.
2. Website and inbox activity flowed into a lead capture process.
3. Zapier created or updated leads in Pipedrive.
4. Leads were categorized by inquiry type, such as general inquiry, specific unit inquiry, price question, showing request, rental office question, or post-showing follow-up.
5. The CRM generated structured tasks, reminders, and follow-up stages.
6. Calendly supported showing bookings and appointment scheduling.
7. Automated customer cadences helped ensure leads received timely follow-up.
8. CRM, website, advertising, and inbox data were consolidated for dashboarding and analysis.
9. Power BI dashboards gave visibility into lead volume, source performance, response workflow, and funnel status.

## Key Features

### 1. CRM Implementation
Implemented Pipedrive as the central sales CRM for La Cité Gatineau. The CRM acted as the operational hub for leads, contacts, stages, showings, follow-ups, and sales tasks.

Portfolio framing:

> Implemented Pipedrive as the source of truth for residential leasing leads, turning scattered website, inbox, phone, and advertising activity into structured pipeline data.

### 2. Lead Capture Automation
Built automations that captured inbound emails and website activity, then created leads in the CRM. This reduced the risk that new inquiries would be missed or handled inconsistently.

Example flow:

- Lead submits a website form, sends an email, requests a showing, or contacts the sales inbox.
- Zapier detects the new inquiry.
- A lead is created in Pipedrive.
- The lead is routed into the correct workflow.
- Follow-up tasks or messages are triggered.

Portfolio framing:

> Automated the conversion of inbound inquiries into CRM leads, helping eliminate unanswered leads and making every inquiry trackable.

### 3. Lead Classification and Follow-Up Logic
Designed workflows to distinguish between different types of leads and inquiries. For example, a pricing question required a different response than a general inquiry or a showing request.

Important logic categories:

- Price question.
- General inquiry.
- Specific inquiry.
- Showing request.
- Rental office question.
- Post-showing follow-up.
- Long-term nurture / six-month re-approach.

Portfolio framing:

> Mapped lead types and follow-up paths so the CRM could support different customer journeys instead of treating every inquiry the same.

### 4. Automated Customer Cadences
Created customer follow-up cadences to support consistent lead nurturing. These cadences helped move leads from inquiry to showing, from showing to follow-up, and from interest to lease signing.

Cadence examples:

- Immediate thank-you / acknowledgement.
- Phone call attempt.
- Email follow-up.
- Showing reminder.
- Post-showing follow-up.
- Long-term re-engagement.

Portfolio framing:

> Built follow-up workflows that standardized sales outreach and reduced dependence on manual memory or one-off email handling.

### 5. Calendly Booking System
Deployed Calendly to support showing bookings and appointment scheduling. This helped convert inbound interest into booked appointments with less back-and-forth.

Portfolio framing:

> Implemented a booking layer that made it easier for prospects to move from interest to scheduled showing.

### 6. Marketing Data Pipeline
Connected marketing channels and funnel data into the broader sales operations system. Data sources included Google Analytics, Meta Ads Manager, Google Ads, website activity, inbox data, and CRM data.

Portfolio framing:

> Connected marketing and sales data so lead generation could be analyzed beyond surface-level traffic metrics and tied more closely to CRM outcomes.

### 7. Power BI Reporting
Built Power BI dashboards to track sales and marketing performance. Dashboards were designed to help understand funnel health, lead sources, response activity, and sales operations performance.

Metrics / dashboard areas:

- Lead volume by source.
- Inbound inquiry volume.
- CRM pipeline status.
- Booking / showing activity.
- Follow-up performance.
- Marketing channel visibility.
- Website and advertising funnel data.
- Sales activity trends.

Portfolio framing:

> Built Power BI dashboards that converted CRM, website, inbox, and advertising data into sales operations visibility.

### 8. Website and Marketing Support
Supported the launch and promotion of the project through website updates, ad media, and video production. This made the role broader than backend operations: it connected the customer-facing funnel to the internal sales process.

Portfolio framing:

> Supported both the front-end marketing funnel and the backend sales operations system, ensuring campaign activity could translate into captured, followed-up, and reported leads.
