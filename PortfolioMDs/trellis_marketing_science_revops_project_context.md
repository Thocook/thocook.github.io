# Portfolio Project Context — Trellis Marketing Science & Revenue Pipeline System

## Project Name
**Trellis — Marketing Science & Revenue Pipeline System**  
**Organization:** Trellis  
**Role / Context:** Marketing science, revenue operations, analytics, and pipeline support  
**Project Type:** B2B SaaS marketing analytics, GA4 analysis, HubSpot pipeline, landing page analytics, paid media reporting, outbound sales enablement

## Portfolio Positioning

This project should be presented as a **marketing science and revenue operations project**, not as a pure Looker dashboard project and not as only campaign management.

The project connected website analytics, landing page performance, paid media sources, HubSpot forms, and HubSpot lead / deal pipelines so marketing and outbound sales teams could better understand which acquisition sources were creating qualified sales opportunities.

Use this core framing:

> Built a marketing science and revenue pipeline system connecting GA4 / Looker website analytics, paid media, landing pages, HubSpot forms, and HubSpot lead / deal pipelines to improve visibility from acquisition source to outbound sales opportunity.

The strongest portfolio angle is that this project sits between **marketing analytics, RevOps, CRM operations, attribution, and sales pipeline visibility**.

## What This Project Was

This was a system for understanding the marketing-to-sales funnel at Trellis.

It brought together:

- **GA4 session and website data**
- **Looker reporting for GA4 and landing page analytics**
- **HubSpot forms**
- **HubSpot contacts / leads**
- **HubSpot deal pipeline**
- **Landing pages**
- **Paid media channels**
- **Outbound sales follow-up workflows**

The goal was to give marketing and outbound teams a clearer view of what happened after traffic arrived: which campaigns generated sessions, which landing pages converted, which forms captured interest, and how those leads moved into HubSpot pipelines used by the outbound team.

## Important Framing Note

Do **not** frame this as if everything lived in Looker.

Better framing:

> Looker was used mainly for GA4 website and landing page analysis. HubSpot served as the operational CRM layer for forms, leads, deals, and outbound follow-up.

Avoid saying:

> Built a complete Looker revenue platform.

Say instead:

> Used Looker as part of a broader marketing science workflow, primarily for GA4 and landing page analytics, while HubSpot handled lead capture and pipeline management.

## Business Problem

Trellis had marketing and outbound activity spread across multiple systems. Paid media, landing pages, website sessions, HubSpot forms, and sales pipeline data could each be viewed separately, but the full path from acquisition source to sales opportunity was harder to understand.

Key problems:

- Website and campaign data lived separately from HubSpot pipeline data.
- Landing page performance was difficult to connect to downstream lead and deal activity.
- Paid media channels needed clearer comparison beyond platform-level metrics.
- HubSpot forms captured interest, but the team needed better visibility into how those form fills became leads and deals.
- Outbound teams needed more useful pipeline context to understand where leads came from.
- Marketing reporting needed to move closer to revenue and pipeline outcomes.

## Solution Overview

The solution was a marketing science and RevOps reporting workflow that connected acquisition, conversion, and CRM pipeline information.

Core components:

- **GA4:** Website session, traffic source, engagement, and landing page data.
- **Looker:** Analysis and visualization layer mainly for GA4 and landing page performance.
- **Landing pages:** Campaign-specific conversion surfaces used to capture demand.
- **HubSpot forms:** Lead capture layer for inbound interest.
- **HubSpot CRM:** Contact, company, lead, and deal management.
- **HubSpot deal pipeline:** Operational pipeline used by outbound and sales teams.
- **Paid media platforms:** Google Ads, Bing Ads, Amazon DSP, and LinkedIn Ads.
- **Outbound workflows:** Sales team follow-up using HubSpot lead and deal records.

Simple workflow:

> Paid Media / Website Traffic → GA4 + Landing Pages → Looker Analysis → HubSpot Forms → HubSpot Leads / Deals → Outbound Team Follow-Up

## System Architecture / Funnel Logic

The project followed a marketing-to-sales funnel structure:

1. Prospects entered through paid media, organic search, direct traffic, or campaign links.
2. GA4 captured session, source / medium, campaign, and landing page data.
3. Looker was used to analyze GA4 website behaviour and landing page performance.
4. Prospects converted through HubSpot forms on landing pages or website pages.
5. Form submissions created or updated HubSpot contact / lead records.
6. Qualified leads could move into HubSpot deal pipelines.
7. The outbound team used HubSpot pipeline context for follow-up and prioritization.
8. Marketing could review which channels and pages appeared to create stronger lead and pipeline activity.

Simple funnel:

> Source → Session → Landing Page → Form Submission → HubSpot Lead → Deal Pipeline → Outbound Follow-Up
